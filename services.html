What is Civil Law in India?

The Civil Law is an unparalleled and certainly the cardinal branch of law for every constitutional and democratic country. Civil Law encompasses of rules and regulations that resolve disputes that are non-criminal in essence. These disputes are between individuals, organizations, companies over private matters meaning the wrong done or the loss inflicted does not involve the State. The court deals with civil cases with an approach to compensate, or pay as remedies. This civil law seeks to resolve disagreements and disputes by the way of providing the due and equitable compensations to the victims, rather than putting emphasis on rigorous punishment or regular punishment to the accused. Civil Law in India is administered by Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (short- CPC) which governs and lays down the process pertaining to various Acts and regulations for example, Indian Contract Act (1872), Trademark Act (1999), Real Estate Regulation Act (2016) and many more.

Section 10 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (i.e. CPC) states that the courts shall, except for the provisions otherwise provided, have jurisdiction to try all cases of a civil nature except suits of which their recognition is either expressly or implicitly barred/ illegal. A suit is of a civil nature if the principal question in the case relates to the determination of a civil right or compensation and rightful remedies. There are certain cases which are barred by particular Acts of the legislature or on the basis of public policy. For example, there are the Acts dealing with the matters relating to cooperative societies, labor disputes, transport matters, etc. Likewise there are suits which are barred on the grounds of public policy, for example, renting a house for the purpose of prostitution, enforcement of an agreement to suppress criminal prosecution, suits to enforce an agreement to suppress an unlawful agreement, etc. these are all illegal practices.

Who is affected Under Civil Law?

Civil law deals with individuals and their private affairs. Organizations, companies, groups having shared intentions with issues such as, breach of contract, cheque bounce, alimony, fraud, property settlement, tort, matrimony etc. It affects anyone wronged under established civil laws i.e. Property Law, Family Law, Law of Contract etc. The Procedure of Justice under Civil Law is to ensure the parties get rightful compensation, remedies and settlement. There isn’t any punishment involved in plain civil suits.
